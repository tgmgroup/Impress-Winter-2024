Question 1

Cordelia: Why did you decide to become an English Teacher?
Mr. Sugawara: Actually, it might sound a little strange, but I have never thought I wanted to be an English teacher.
Cordelia: Wow, really?
Mr. Sugawara: Yeah, I mean, when I was a university student I didn’t major in English.
Cordelia: Oh, wow!
Mr. Sugawara: Yes, I studied to be an elementary school teacher.
Cordelia: Oh, I see.
Mr. Sugawara: Yes. So, I took the entrance exam to be a teacher for elementary school teachers, and I passed it.
Cordelia: Oh, really?
Mr. Sugawara: Yeah, so I thought that I could be an elementary school teacher.
Cordelia: Uh-huh.
Mr. Sugawara: But I don’t know why I must start my career as a junior high school teacher. So that’s why it was kind of compulsory. I had no choice.
Cordelia: *laughs* I see.
Mr. Sugawara: But, yeah, now I really enjoy my job and I’m proud of my job. So now I can be thankful for my fate or destiny or something.
Cordelia: I see. I think you’re a really good English teacher, so I think this job suits you a lot.
Mr. Sugawara: Oh, thank you so much.

Question 4

Cordelia: Ok, why do you think English is important or useful for us?

Mr. Sugawara: Maybe I have two opinions. The first one is that speaking English is kind of a communication tool. So as I said, if we can speak English, we can go anywhere. And we can talk to a lot of people, and I can touch many cultures or something. But, as you know, in Japan maybe we don’t have to speak English to live. But if we can learn another language, we can learn another culture. And we can get new viewpoints to see the world. So I think this is just my opinion, but all Japanese should study English.

Cordelia: I see.
